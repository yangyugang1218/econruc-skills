# Introduction Diagnostics

Use this checklist to audit an economics introduction draft.

## Fast diagnosis

| Question | Good sign | Warning sign |
|---|---|---|
| What is the economic question? | Can be stated in one sentence with a margin and outcome | The draft begins with broad background for several paragraphs |
| Why should economists care? | Links to allocation, welfare, incentives, distribution, productivity, prices, risk, or policy tradeoffs | Only says the topic is important or timely |
| What is unresolved? | Names a specific empirical, theoretical, measurement, or identification gap | Says simply that prior work is limited |
| Why is the question hard? | Explains endogeneity, selection, missing counterfactual, measurement, equilibrium, or mechanism problem | No identification problem appears before the design |
| What does the paper do? | Setting, data, and design are clear | The reader cannot tell what variation identifies the result |
| What does the paper find? | Gives magnitude and benchmark | Only says positive/negative/significant |
| What is the contribution? | Separates substantive, data/design, and literature contributions | Lists literatures without saying what changes |

## Revision moves

- Move the research question into the first or second paragraph.
- Replace broad policy motivation with a specific economic tradeoff.
- Add one sentence explaining why naive comparisons are biased.
- State the identifying variation before naming the estimator.
- Convert statistical significance into economic magnitude.
- Split contribution paragraphs by literature or contribution type.
- Replace `fills a gap` with a precise statement of what the paper teaches.
- Add boundary language if the design is local or the sample is special.

## Common introduction failures

### Background swamp

Problem: the introduction spends too long on history, policy, or descriptive trends.

Fix: keep only the institutional facts needed to understand the variation, measurement, or stakes.

### Literature-first opening

Problem: the first substantive paragraph is a literature review.

Fix: start with the economic question, then use literature to explain what remains unresolved.

### Design appears too late

Problem: the reader learns the empirical strategy only after several pages.

Fix: introduce the setting and identification logic soon after the puzzle.

### Contribution inflation

Problem: claims are broader than the evidence.

Fix: use local, design-consistent wording and state external-validity boundaries.

### Mechanism overclaim

Problem: heterogeneity or mediation evidence is described as proof of mechanism.

Fix: distinguish evidence consistent with a channel from a separately identified mechanism.

## Chinese draft checks

- 是否有明确的“问题意识”，而不是只写政策背景？
- 是否说明已有研究为什么未能回答本文问题？
- 是否交代识别困难和本文的切入点？
- 是否把“显著影响”改写为有经济含义的数量解释？
- 是否将边际贡献分为问题、数据/识别、机制/政策三类？
- 是否避免“具有重要理论和现实意义”等空泛表达？
