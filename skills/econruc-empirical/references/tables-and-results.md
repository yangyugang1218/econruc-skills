# Tables and Results

## Table logic

Economics tables should progress from simple to credible to interpretable:

1. Descriptive statistics or balance.
2. Baseline estimates.
3. Alternative specifications.
4. Robustness and placebo.
5. Mechanism or heterogeneity.

## Results prose

Paragraph template:

`Column [x] reports [specification]. The coefficient on [variable] is [estimate] ([standard error]), implying [economic magnitude]. Adding [controls/fixed effects] changes the estimate by [amount], suggesting [interpretation].`

## Magnitudes

Always translate:

- percentage points versus percent
- log points versus percent
- standard deviations
- monetary units
- baseline mean comparisons

## Table notes

Include:

- unit of observation
- sample and period
- fixed effects
- controls
- clustering
- weights
- outcome definition

## Avoid

- discussing significance stars without magnitude
- calling every column a robustness check
- hiding sample changes
- over-interpreting mechanism tables
