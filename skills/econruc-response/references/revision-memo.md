# Revision Memo

## Editor letter structure

1. Thank the editor.
2. Summarize major revisions in 3-5 bullets.
3. Highlight identification or robustness changes first.
4. Mention exposition/literature changes second.
5. Close with confidence but not overstatement.

Template:

`We thank you and the referees for the constructive guidance. The revision makes four main changes. First, ... Second, ... Third, ... Fourth, ... We believe these revisions clarify the paper's identification, sharpen its contribution, and make the scope of the findings more transparent.`

## Change-summary language

- `We have added...`
- `We have revised...`
- `We now report...`
- `We now discuss...`
- `We have softened the claim from [old] to [new].`

## Risk checks

- Do not claim a new result without the estimate.
- Do not say `all concerns are resolved`.
- Do not bury identification changes under exposition changes.
- Do not promise future work as if it were current evidence.
