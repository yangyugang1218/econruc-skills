# Paper to Slides

## Extraction pass

Read the paper and extract:

- language choice: Chinese or English deck
- research question
- one-sentence answer
- economic stakes
- contribution to literatures
- setting and institution
- data and sample
- identification/model
- main quantitative result
- mechanism
- robustness
- limitations
- figures/tables worth showing

Then add an economist extraction pass:

- headline magnitudes that can anchor the first 10-12 slides
- the core economic tension, not only the paper topic
- exact empirical, theoretical, or structural object being estimated
- competing explanations that a seminar audience will raise
- identification threats, modeling assumptions, and what evidence addresses them
- literature contrast in the form "standard view -> this paper's departure"
- welfare, policy, or market-design trade-off
- which results deserve main-slide visuals and which belong in backup

## Conversion rules

Every paper deck must start with an Introduction section, even if the paper does not have a section explicitly called `Introduction`.

The Introduction section must contain at least these three slides:

- `Research motivation`: why the economic margin matters; not generic background
- `Research question`: the precise question, comparison, and unresolved problem
- `Research contribution`: separate substantive contribution, design/data/model contribution, and literature/policy contribution

The rest of the paper introduction becomes:

- puzzle or gap slide
- preview slide

Literature review becomes:

- one contribution slide
- optional backup literature map

Data section becomes:

- one data source slide
- one measurement/sample slide
- one descriptive fact slide if it motivates design

Empirical strategy/model section becomes:

- intuition slide first
- equation slide only if audience needs the formal object
- identifying assumption or model mechanism slide

Results become:

- one slide per major claim
- figure or selected table fragment as evidence
- takeaway bullets on the side
- selected magnitudes rather than a full regression table whenever possible

Full paper decks should include at least one evidence-bearing slide within the first 10-12 slides. This can be a clean statistic, a stylized but faithful figure, or a selected result table. A prototype may begin with architecture, but a final seminar deck must earn credibility early.

Robustness becomes:

- main slide only for threats that matter
- backup for completeness

Conclusion becomes:

- central answer
- economic implication
- boundary

## What not to do

- Do not paste paragraphs from the paper.
- Do not show every regression table.
- Do not spend more slides on methods than the audience needs to trust the main result.
- Do not hide the research question until slide 10.
- Do not leave figures with paper-sized fonts.
- Do not mix Chinese and English slide prose unless bilingual output is explicitly requested.
- Do not use the paper's section headings mechanically if they make a poor talk structure.
- Do not skip the Introduction section just because the source paper embeds motivation and contribution across several paragraphs.
- Do not rely on generic mechanism slides for too long; economists expect magnitudes, variation, or model objects quickly.
- Do not let stylized figures replace the paper's evidence when the original figure or reconstructed data can be shown cleanly.

## Speaker notes logic

For each slide, know:

- what the audience should learn
- why this slide appears now
- what question it answers from the previous slide
- what question it sets up next
