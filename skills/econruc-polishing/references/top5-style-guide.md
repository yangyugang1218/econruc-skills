# Top5 Economics Style Guide

Use for polishing economics prose toward AER/QJE/Econometrica/JPE/REStud and strong field-journal style.

## Sentence principles

- Prefer short first sentences that state the economic object.
- Put the design near the claim.
- Put magnitudes in economic units.
- Make the contribution sound earned, not advertised.
- Use fewer adjectives and more nouns: setting, variation, estimator, mechanism, margin.

## Verb discipline

Use:

- `study`, `estimate`, `document`, `show`, `provide evidence`, `compare`, `exploit`, `leverage`, `test`, `examine`.

Use cautiously:

- `demonstrate`: when evidence is strong.
- `suggest`: for mechanism or interpretation.
- `cause`: only with credible causal design and explicit assumptions.
- `prove`: almost never in empirical prose.

## Common rewrites

Weak:

`This paper has important theoretical and practical significance for promoting high-quality economic development.`

Stronger:

`The results clarify how [policy] changes [economic margin], a central channel through which [market/policy] can affect [welfare/outcome].`

Weak:

`The policy significantly promotes firm innovation.`

Stronger:

`The policy increases patent applications by 8 percent relative to the pre-policy mean.`

Weak:

`This paper fills a gap in the literature.`

Stronger:

`The paper contributes to this literature by isolating [margin] using [source of variation], which prior studies could not separately observe.`

## Paragraph checks

Every paragraph should answer one question:

- What is the claim?
- What evidence supports it?
- What design or model makes it credible?
- What does the magnitude mean?
- What is the boundary?

If a paragraph has no coefficient, mechanism, design, literature relation, or interpretation, it may be filler.
