# Report Architecture

## Report types

### Policy analysis report

Use when the task concerns a policy problem, policy design, policy evaluation, or regulatory choice.

Default structure:

1. Executive summary.
2. Policy question and scope.
3. Institutional background.
4. Key facts and data.
5. Analysis of causes, constraints, or effects.
6. Policy options.
7. Recommendation and implementation conditions.
8. Risks and monitoring indicators.
9. Sources and appendix.

### Industry research report

Use when the task concerns a sector, market, industrial chain, technology, or regional industry.

Default structure:

1. Executive summary.
2. Industry definition and scope.
3. Market size and growth.
4. Supply-demand structure.
5. Competitive landscape or regional pattern.
6. Policy and technology drivers.
7. Key risks.
8. Outlook or scenarios.
9. Source notes.

### Consulting-style report

Use when the user needs a decision-oriented, client-facing analytical report.

Default structure:

1. Executive answer.
2. Context and decision question.
3. Diagnostic findings.
4. Evidence exhibits.
5. Strategic options.
6. Recommended path.
7. Implementation roadmap.
8. Risks and assumptions.

## Executive summary rules

- Put conclusions before background.
- Each bullet should contain one claim and, when possible, one sourced magnitude.
- Avoid vague conclusions such as `发展空间巨大` unless the report later proves them.
- Do not introduce numbers in the executive summary that are absent from the body/source register.

## Headline style

Weak:

`行业发展现状`

Better:

`需求扩张放缓后，行业增长主要转向结构升级`

Weak:

`政策建议`

Better:

`政策重点应从规模扩张转向并网消纳和成本传导`
