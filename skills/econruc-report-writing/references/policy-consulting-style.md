# Policy and Consulting Style

## Style principles

- Write for decision-makers, not only for academic readers.
- Put the answer before the explanation.
- Keep section headings claim-first.
- Translate data into implications, but do not overclaim.
- Use risk language explicitly.

## Recommendation language

Strong recommendation:

`建议优先推进...，前提是...，并通过...指标跟踪效果。`

Bounded recommendation:

`在现有数据约束下，更稳妥的方案是...；若后续...发生变化，应重新评估。`

Avoid:

- `必须全面推进`
- `必将带来`
- `前景无限`
- `显著提升` without evidence

## Risk matrix

Use a matrix when recommendations depend on uncertainty.

| Risk | Mechanism | Evidence | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

## Scenario framing

Use scenarios when forecasts are necessary but uncertain:

- Baseline: current policy and trend continue.
- Upside: stronger demand/policy/technology improvement.
- Downside: demand weakens, policy delay, cost pressure, external shock.

Always state assumptions behind each scenario.

## Tone

Chinese:

- clear, restrained, direct
- less slogan, more mechanism
- concise recommendations with conditions

English:

- concise executive prose
- evidence first
- clear distinction between observation, inference, and recommendation
