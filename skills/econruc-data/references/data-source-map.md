# Economics Data Source Map

## Source hierarchy

Prefer:

1. Official statistical agencies and regulator data.
2. Replication packages and official APIs.
3. International databases with documented methodology.
4. Commercial databases only when the user has access and licensing is clear.
5. Scraped sources only when stable and legally appropriate.

## China-focused sources

- National Bureau of Statistics: macro, industry, regional, census, yearbooks.
- PBOC/SAFE/CSRC/NAFMII: finance, monetary, securities, exchange, bond markets.
- Ministry datasets: education, health, transport, ecology, commerce, customs.
- CSMAR/WIND/RESSET/CCER: listed firms, finance, accounting, patents, executives.
- China City Statistical Yearbook: city panels.
- Customs and trade databases: firm-product-destination trade when available.
- CNIPA and patent databases: patents, citations, applicants.
- CFPS/CHFS/CHARLS/CGSS/CLDS: household and labor surveys.

## International sources

- World Bank WDI, IMF, OECD, UN Comtrade, IEA, Ember, IRENA, Our World in Data.
- FRED, BLS, BEA, Census, Eurostat.
- Compustat, CRSP, Orbis, Refinitiv, PitchBook where licensed.

## Selection criteria

For each source, record:

- coverage
- unit and frequency
- update date
- definition changes
- access method
- license constraints
- citation format
- merge keys
