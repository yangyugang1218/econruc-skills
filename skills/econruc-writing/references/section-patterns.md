# Economics Section Patterns

## Title

Good title forms:

- `[Policy/Shock] and [Outcome]: Evidence from [Setting]`
- `The [Economic Effect] of [Policy/Institution]`
- `[Mechanism] in [Market/Population]: Evidence from [Design]`
- `When [Incentive/Constraint] Changes: [Outcome] in [Setting]`

Avoid grant-like titles:

- `Research on the Impact Mechanism of...`
- `A Study of High-Quality Development...`
- `An Empirical Analysis Based on...`

## Abstract paragraph moves

1. `We study...`
2. `We exploit/use/link...`
3. `We find...`
4. `The effect is concentrated among...`
5. `The evidence is consistent with...`
6. `The results contribute to...`

## Introduction contribution paragraph

Template:

`This paper contributes to three literatures. First, it contributes to work on [literature] by [margin]. Second, it adds to research on [literature] by [data/design/mechanism]. Third, it speaks to policy debates on [policy] by showing [bounded implication].`

## Results opening

Template:

`We begin by estimating the effect of [treatment] on [outcome]. The baseline specification compares [treated] with [comparison group] before and after [shock], controlling for [fixed effects/controls]. The estimate is [coefficient], implying [economic magnitude].`

## Mechanism paragraph

Template:

`We next examine whether [channel] helps explain the baseline effect. If this channel is operative, we should observe [testable implication]. Consistent with this prediction, [evidence]. This pattern suggests that [interpretation], although it does not rule out [alternative].`

## Limitation paragraph

Template:

`Several limitations qualify the interpretation. First, [design boundary]. Second, [measurement boundary]. Third, [external validity boundary]. These limitations do not overturn [main evidence], but they narrow the claim to [bounded claim].`
